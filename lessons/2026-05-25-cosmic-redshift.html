A single light wave caught in flight while space expands. A green line emitted at 550 nm (z = 0, the present) reddens and stretches as you wind the universe back to a smaller, hotter past. The observed wavelength grows by the factor 1 + z, the marker on the spectrum bar slides toward the red edge and out into the infrared, and the temperature of the photon bath that carries the light rises by the same 1 + z. Wavelength and temperature stretch together because they are one stretch.
