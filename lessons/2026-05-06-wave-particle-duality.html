Electron kinetic energy K (slider, log scale 1 eV to 1000 eV) sets the de Broglie wavelength λ = h/√(2 m_e K). The schematic shows electrons hitting a nickel crystal at normal incidence; the surface row spacing is d = 2.15 Å (green marker on the lower scale). The first-order Bragg condition d sin φ = λ predicts the angle φ at which the diffracted peak emerges. At 54 eV (Davisson 1927) the answer is φ ≈ 51°, exactly the angle of the strongest peak in his data. Below the threshold where λ > d (around 32 eV), no first-order diffraction is geometrically possible at all.
