Three colored rays (the hydrogen F line at 486 nm in blue, the helium d line at 588 nm in green, the hydrogen C line at 656 nm in red) enter from the left and refract through a thin lens. The chromatic spread of the foci is shown 8× exaggerated so the F-C gap is visible; the readout reports the true (un-magnified) focal lengths in mm. Toggle between singlet and doublet. The singlet (BK7) gives three clearly separated foci. The doublet (BK7 cemented to a flint of Abbe number V flint) collapses the red and blue foci back onto the green; sliding V flint up toward V crown = 64.2 shrinks the Abbe contrast and forces both elements to carry impossibly large individual powers, and the doublet falls apart at the right edge of the slider.
