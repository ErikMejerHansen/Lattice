Thermal-noise voltage across a resistor R at temperature T, viewed through a 10 kHz bandwidth. The two sliders sweep T from 4 K (liquid helium) to about 3000 K (a warm cathode), and R from 10 Ω to 10 MΩ. The vertical axis is auto-scaled to ±3 V_rms so the trace shape stays comparable across settings; the ±3σ, ±σ, and 0 tick labels report the actual voltage in fV, pV, nV, μV, or mV. Watch how the axis labels change (V_rms scales as √R and as √T) even though the trace itself looks statistically identical. Cool from 300 K to 4 K and the axis labels drop by about a factor of 8.7; boost R by a decade and they climb by √10.
