The shaded column on the left is the conduction band of the metal, filled up to the Fermi energy EF. At nonzero temperature the Fermi-Dirac distribution f(E,T) = 1 / (1 + exp((E − EF)/kT)) smears the cutoff over a few kT around EF; the faint tail just above EF is the few electrons sitting above the Fermi level. The dashed line is the vacuum level Evac, set by the chosen metal's work function φ = Evac − EF. Electrons in the Fermi-Dirac tail above Evac can escape into the vacuum, giving the thermionic emission current J = AT² exp(−φ/kT). Switch metals to see φ change by a factor of three; J spans fifteen orders of magnitude across the same temperature.
