Hydrogen energy levels E_n = −13.6 eV / n² as horizontal rules, with the ionization limit at 0 eV. Pick a series with the buttons (which level the electron lands on), then drag the slider to scan the upper level n₂ the electron is falling from. The red arrow shows the chosen transition. The spectrum bar at the bottom marks the emitted wavelength on a logarithmic axis from 80 nm to 4000 nm, with the visible window (380 to 750 nm) shaded with a rainbow gradient. Only the Balmer series (n₁ = 2) puts most of its lines inside the visible band; Lyman lives in the UV, Paschen in the IR.
