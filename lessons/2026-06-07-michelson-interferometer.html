An interactive Michelson. Drag M₂ in nanometers and watch (a) the path difference Δd = 2(L₂ − L₁) accumulate at twice the mirror displacement, (b) the phase Δφ = 4πΔx/λ advance, and (c) the detector intensity I/I_max = cos²(Δφ/2) cycle between bright and dark. One bright-to-bright fringe is Δx = λ/2 of mirror travel and Δd = λ of round-trip path difference. Switch the source between sodium (589 nm) and Nd:YAG (1064 nm) to see the same mirror motion produce different fringe counts: a longer-wavelength source needs a larger Δx to advance the phase by 2π.
