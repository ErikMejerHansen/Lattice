A Kelvin-probe schematic. Two plates face each other with a fixed vacuum-gap contact potential V_CPD = +0.70 V (say, a gold tip against an aluminum sample). The top plate vibrates at frequency omega, so the gap capacitance C(t) sweeps between C_min and C_max. The right panel plots the circuit current I(t) = (V_CPD + V_ext) dC/dt at the current V_ext setting. Move the slider until the AC amplitude collapses; the null occurs at V_ext = -V_CPD = -0.70 V, and that null bias IS the measurement of V_CPD, with no absolute-current calibration needed.
