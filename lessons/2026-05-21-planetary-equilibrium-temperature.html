Equilibrium temperature against orbital distance, T_eq ∝ d−1/2. Drag the distance slider to slide the marker along the curve; drag the albedo slider to darken or brighten the planet, lifting or lowering the whole curve. Faint ticks mark the real planets at their own distances. The curve sags gently: a four-fold drop in distance-squared sunlight, run through a fourth root, becomes only a square-root drop in temperature, which is why Mars near 1.5 AU is cold but not frozen solid.
