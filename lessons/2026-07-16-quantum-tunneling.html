A rectangular barrier of width L and height U with a particle of total energy E incident from the left. The shaded rectangle is the barrier, the dashed horizontal line at height E is the energy level, and the red curve is the real part of the wave function ψ(x) drawn around that E line. Outside the barrier ψ oscillates with wavenumber k = √(2mE)/ℏ. Inside it decays exponentially with κ = √(2m(U−E))/ℏ. On the far side it oscillates at amplitude √T. Slide L or E/U and watch T change exponentially. The T readout is the exact formula T = 1/(1 + sinh²(κL) · U²/(4E(U−E))); e^(−2κL) is the large-κL approximation Gamow used.
