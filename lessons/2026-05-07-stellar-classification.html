Drag the temperature slider. The top panel shows two curves: the fraction of hydrogen ionized (rust) and the Balmer absorption strength (dark), normalized to its own peak. Balmer strength is the product of (fraction neutral) and (fraction of neutral hydrogen sitting in n=2, ready to absorb), so it dies on the cool side because n=2 is empty and dies on the hot side because hydrogen has been stripped. The bottom strip is the OBAFGKM sequence painted in roughly the color a star at that temperature actually shows; the rust pin marks where your slider is.
