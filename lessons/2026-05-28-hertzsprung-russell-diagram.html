A populated H-R diagram. Hot temperatures left, luminosities up, both logarithmic. The dense diagonal is the main sequence; the cloud upper right is the red-giant and supergiant region; the thin band lower left is the white-dwarf cooling sequence. Named stars are marked. Drag the mass slider to place a zero-age main-sequence star (filled red dot) and read its luminosity, surface temperature, radius, and remaining hydrogen-burning lifetime. The faded red dots and dashed line trace the path the same star will walk after the main sequence: up onto the giant branch, then down to its endpoint as a white dwarf or, above about 8 solar masses, as a supernova.
