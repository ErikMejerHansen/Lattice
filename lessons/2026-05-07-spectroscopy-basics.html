The strip is the visible spectrum from 380 to 740 nm, drawn with the rough hue the eye assigns each wavelength. Tap an element to drop its absorption fingerprint into the beam, as if you placed that cool vapor between yourself and a hot continuum source. Tap again to remove it. The bottom panel lists every line you have switched on. Mix species to see why the real solar spectrum, with thousands of lines from dozens of elements, looks the way it does.
