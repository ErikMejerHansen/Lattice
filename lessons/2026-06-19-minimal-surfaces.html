Two coaxial rings of radius R sit at heights z = ±h. Drag h/R to pull them apart. Below h/R ≈ 0.528 the catenoid is the global minimum (less area than the two disks). Between 0.528 and 0.6627 the catenoid still exists but is metastable, and a real soap film at this ratio will snap on perturbation. Above 0.6627 no catenoid exists; the film must collapse to two flat disks (Goldschmidt's 1831 discontinuous solution).
