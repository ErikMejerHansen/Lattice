A de Broglie matter wave wrapped around a Bohr orbit. The dashed circle is the orbit, the dark dot is the proton, and the rust curve is the electron's matter wave, with its amplitude shown as a radial displacement from the orbit. The slider sets the number of wavelengths n that fit around the circumference. The green dot marks where the wave starts (at the right, θ = 0); the second dot, also at θ = 0 but now reached after one full lap, marks where the wave ends. At integer n the end lands on the start in phase, the wave closes, and the orbit is a standing wave (resonant). At non-integer n the end misses the start, the dashed segment between them is the residual mismatch, and on successive laps the wave interferes destructively with itself.
