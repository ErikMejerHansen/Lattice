Slide the principal quantum number n from 2 to 100. The solid line is the quantum jump frequency ν_q(n → n−1) between adjacent hydrogen levels; the dashed line is the classical Kepler orbital frequency ν_cl(n) of an electron on the n-th Bohr orbit. Both fall as 1/n³ on the log-y plot. The ratio ν_q / ν_cl (shown in the readout) starts at 3 for n = 2 and drops toward 1 as n grows. At n ≈ 100 the two frequencies agree to about a percent. This is Bohr's correspondence principle in its narrowest form: at large n, quantum jumps between adjacent levels sound like the classical orbital frequency.
