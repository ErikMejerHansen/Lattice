Top-down orthographic-from-north-pole view of the unit sphere. The accent latitude circle sits at colatitude θ (angle from the pole). At fifteen evenly spaced points along the loop, the parallel-transported tangent vector is drawn in gray; the dark arrow is the starting vector and the accent arrow is the same vector after one full circuit, both at the same physical point. The angle between them is the holonomy 2π(1 − cos θ), the Gauss-Bonnet integral of K = 1 over the polar cap of solid angle 2π(1 − cos θ). At θ → 0 the holonomy vanishes (a tiny loop encloses no area); at θ = 90° (equator) it is 2π, a full rotation that returns the vector to itself.
