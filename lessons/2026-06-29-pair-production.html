A 1.022 MeV photon is the minimum that can become an electron and a positron at rest. Slide hν below threshold and energy conservation alone forbids the conversion. Slide it above threshold and the pair appears at the nucleus, with track radii proportional to each particle's momentum. Hide the nucleus and even an 5 MeV photon passes through unconverted: a free photon in vacuum cannot turn into a pair because its momentum has no place to be deposited. The tiny arrow on the nucleus, when shown, is the nuclear recoil that balances the books.
