Drag the sag slider. The accent curve is the true catenary; the dashed grey curve is the parabola y = (sag) · (x/L)² fitted to the same two supports and the same lowest point. At small sag the two are visually identical (the regime in which Galileo's eye accepted the parabola in 1638). As sag grows the parabola lifts off the catenary in the middle: the cosh's fourth-order term has begun to matter.
