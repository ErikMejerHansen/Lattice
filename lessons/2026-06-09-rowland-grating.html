The Rowland circle (dashed) is the locus on which the slit S, the concave grating G, and the entire focused spectrum simultaneously sit. The grating is fixed at the bottom of the circle and its normal runs along the vertical dashed line. Drag the α slider to place the slit on the circle at incidence angle α from the normal; the colored dots are the focused images of monochromatic wavelengths from 400 nm (violet) to 700 nm (red), each computed from d(sin α + sin β) = mλ with grating spacing d = 1.5 μm. The pale ray from the slit to G is the incoming light; the colored rays from G to each focus point are the diffracted orders. No lens appears anywhere; the curvature of the grating handles all the focusing. Toggle m to see the same wavelengths land at new angles in higher orders and watch the spectrum stretch around the circle.
