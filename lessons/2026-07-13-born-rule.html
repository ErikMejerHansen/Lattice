Two Gaussian wave packets, one centered at x = −2 and one at x = +2, are added with a relative phase Δφ. The upper panel shows the real and imaginary parts of the sum ψ(x). The lower panel shows |ψ(x)|² and the position samples drawn from it: each click of Sample draws one x value from the probability density and drops a dot. Sweep Δφ to watch two packets interfere in ψ (crest-crest and crest-trough patterns visible in the amplitude) and correspondingly bunch or spread in |ψ|². The single-shot dots are Born's answer to Schrödinger's smeared cloud: every measurement returns one point, and only the accumulation of many measurements traces out the density.
