Copper's Fermi energy E_F ≈ 7 eV is held fixed, and the chemical potential μ is taken equal to E_F (the T ≪ T_F approximation, correct to order (T/T_F)²). Slide T on a log scale from 30 K up to 300,000 K. Below the Fermi temperature T_F ≈ 82,000 K the distribution is a nearly perfect step with a k_B T-wide smear at the edge, and only the shaded band participates in transport. Above T_F the smear swallows the whole distribution and the Fermi-Dirac curve converges onto the classical Boltzmann tail.
