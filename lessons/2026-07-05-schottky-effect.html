The escape barrier for an electron above a metal surface, U(z) = -e²/(16πε₀z) - eEz. The blue dashed line marks the Fermi level (−φ = −4.5 eV, a bare tungsten cathode). The orange curve is the total tilted potential. The barrier peak walks in toward the surface and drops in height as the field E climbs. The readout gives z_max, Δφ (the barrier reduction), and the current enhancement exp(Δφ/kT) at T = 2000 K. Past E ~ 3·10⁹ V/m, Δφ becomes comparable to φ and the classical over-the-top picture breaks down.
