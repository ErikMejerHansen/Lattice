The horseshoe is the CIE 1931 (x, y) chromaticity diagram. Pure spectral wavelengths sit on the outside curve, labeled in nanometers. The dashed bottom edge is the line of purples, mixtures of violet and red that no single wavelength can produce. The rust curve is the Planckian locus, the chromaticity trace of a blackbody at every temperature. Slide T to walk the marker from a red-hot 1500 K, through tungsten illuminant A near 2856 K, through noon daylight at D65 (6504 K), up into the sky-blue corner near 20000 K. The gray dot at E is the equal-energy white where x = y = 1/3.
