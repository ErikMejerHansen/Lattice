An echellogram on a 4k by 4k CCD. Each horizontal stripe is one diffraction order m of the echelle; the cross-disperser separates orders along the vertical axis. Along each stripe the high echelle dispersion paints the free spectral range FSR = λ/m of that order as a thin rainbow. The blaze condition 2d sin θ_B = mλ_B sets which orders are bright; the slider for θ_B sweeps the blaze across the visible band, and the slider for line spacing d trades line count for order. The dashed red mark is the chosen spectral line (sodium D₂ by default), visible jumping from order to order as the blaze geometry shifts. The R = mN readout updates live with N held at 16,000 lines.
