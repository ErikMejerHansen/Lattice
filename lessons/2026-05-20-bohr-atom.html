An electron's de Broglie wave drawn as a radial ripple around its circular orbit (the dashed ring). The slider sets how many wavelengths span the circumference. The faint second-lap segment shows where the wave goes on its next trip around: when the count is a whole integer it lies exactly on top of the first lap (a standing wave, reinforcing itself) and the seam closes; when it is not an integer the second lap peels away from the first and the wave cancels itself by interference, so no stable orbit exists. Only integer settings survive, which is precisely Bohr's rule L = nℏ.
