A spin-½ state on the Bloch sphere at polar angle θ (from +z) and azimuth φ. Left panel is the sphere with the state drawn as a red vector. Right panel compares Robertson's left side (ΔJ_x·ΔJ_y, orange) against its right side (½|⟨[Ĵ_x, Ĵ_y]⟩| = (ℏ/2)|⟨Ĵ_z⟩|, red), both in units of ℏ². At the poles the bound is saturated at ℏ²/4. On the equator the right side goes to zero and the bound goes vacuous. Sliding φ away from the meridians introduces Schrödinger's covariance term, which lifts the left side above the right; the tan bar underneath is the gap LHS − RHS.
