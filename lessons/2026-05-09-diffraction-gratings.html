N = 2
Multi-slit intensity I(φ) / N² as a function of inter-slit phase φ = (2π d sin θ) / λ. Principal maxima sit at φ = 2πm (orders m = -1, 0, +1 shown), independent of N. Drag N from 2 (Young's two slits) to 30. Watch the principal maxima narrow (FWHM ∝ 1/N) and the secondary bumps between them flatten. By N = 20 the secondary maxima are below 1% of the principal heights. Extrapolate to Fraunhofer's 12,000 illuminated rulings, where the spikes have a width measured in arcseconds, and you have a spectrometer.
