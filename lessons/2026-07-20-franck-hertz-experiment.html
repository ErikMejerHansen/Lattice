Top: anode current I(V) as V sweeps 0 to 20 volts. Dips fall at V ≈ 4.9, 9.8, 14.7, 19.6 V, the multiples of the mercury 6³P₁ excitation energy. Bottom: schematic of a single electron drifting from cathode (left) to grid (right), with its kinetic energy plotted along the way. Each time the kinetic energy reaches 4.9 eV, an inelastic collision with a mercury atom pulls it back to zero (sawtooth pattern). Only electrons with kinetic energy above eV_r ≈ 0.5 eV at the grid can clear the retarding hill and reach the anode.
