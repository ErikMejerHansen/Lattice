Photons of frequency ν strike a clean alkali-metal cathode (top schematic). Below the threshold ν₀ = φ/h, no electrons escape, no matter how many photons arrive. Above the threshold, each absorbed photon ejects one electron with maximum kinetic energy K_max = hν − φ. The plot underneath shows three metals at once: parallel lines, same slope h, different intercepts φ. Switch metals to see Millikan's central observation, the geometry he traced in 1916 to one part in a hundred.
