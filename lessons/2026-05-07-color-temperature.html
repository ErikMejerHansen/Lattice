Drag the slider. The swatch is the rough perceived color of an ideal blackbody at temperature T (Tanner Helland's standard approximation). The Planck curve below it is normalized to its own peak; the rust band marks the visible window from 380 to 740 nm, and the dashed line is λmax from Wien's law. A 2700 K incandescent puts only a sliver of its curve in the visible window; a 6500 K daylight source has its peak just past the blue edge.
