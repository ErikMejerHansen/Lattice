An incoming photon of wavelength λ strikes a stationary electron at the origin. Drag the lower slider to choose the photon's scattering angle θ. The scattered wavelength follows λ' = λ + (h/mec)(1 − cos θ); the recoil electron's angle φ and kinetic energy fall out of energy and momentum conservation. The three arrows are drawn to scale in momentum. Sweep θ from 0° to 180° and watch Δλ trace the (1 − cos θ) curve Compton saw in his graphite spectra.
