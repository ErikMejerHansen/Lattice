A Poisson click stream. The slider sets the mean arrival rate r̄ over ten decades (10² to 10¹² electrons per second, or 16 aA to 160 nA of current). The top strip shows individual arrivals as vertical ticks over a scrolling 100-ms window; when the rate exceeds what the eye can resolve, the ticks blur into a solid band. The lower panel plots the smoothed current I(t) through a single-pole filter of time constant τ = 5 ms (noise bandwidth Δf = 100 Hz), with a dashed mean line and a shaded ±σ band whose width is 2√(2eIΔf). Watch the mean grow linearly with r̄ while the band width grows only as √r̄: the fractional noise δI/I collapses as 1/√r̄, which is why brighter photodiodes are quieter photodiodes.
