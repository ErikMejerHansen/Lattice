The two non-trivial Schwarzschild metric coefficients plotted against r/r_s, with the accent solid curve −g_tt = 1 − r_s/r and the accent dashed curve g_rr = 1/(1 − r_s/r). The shaded band r < r_s is the interior of the event horizon, where the sign of g_tt flips and the radial coordinate becomes timelike. Vertical markers at r = r_s (horizon), r = 1.5 r_s (photon sphere, light orbits unstably), and r = 3 r_s (ISCO, smallest stable massive-particle orbit). Drag the slider to move a probe; the readout shows the local time-dilation factor 1/√(1 − r_s/r) (how much slower a stationary local clock runs compared to a distant clock) and the radial-ruler stretch √(g_rr) (how much longer a stationary local ruler measures the same Δr as a distant ruler). r in km uses the solar-mass r_s = 2.95 km; for other masses scale linearly.
