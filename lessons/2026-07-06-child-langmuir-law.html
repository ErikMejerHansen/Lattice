The potential V(x) inside a planar vacuum diode. The solid rust curve is the space-charge-limited profile V₀(x/d)^{4/3} that Child and Langmuir derived; note how flat it is at the cathode, where Langmuir's boundary condition pins the surface field to zero. The dashed cream line is what V(x) would look like in the same gap with no electrons in flight (linear, uniform field V₀/d). The readout shows the Child-Langmuir current density J for this V₀ and d, along with the field at the anode (which is 4V₀/(3d) in the space-charge case, one-third larger than the naive V₀/d), and the perveance per unit area K = J/V₀^{3/2}.
