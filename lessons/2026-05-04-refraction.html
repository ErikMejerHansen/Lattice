A source A in a fast medium (n₁ = 1.00) above the interface, target B in a slow medium (n₂, adjustable) below. Drag the crossing point P along the interface and watch the total travel time T(x) plotted underneath. The straight-line path is rarely the fastest; the minimum-time path bends, and the dashed marker on the time curve is the Fermat optimum, exactly where n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂. Push n₂ higher to see denser media bend the path harder.
