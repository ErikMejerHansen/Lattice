The plot shows ψ(x,t) = 2A cos((Δk/2)x - (Δω/2)t) cos(k̄ x - ω̄ t), the sum of two cosines with wavenumbers k̄ ± Δk/2 and frequencies ω̄ ± Δω/2. The orange envelope rides on the steel-blue carrier. Two markers follow the same packet over time: the orange dot tracks one envelope maximum (group), the blue dot tracks one carrier crest (phase). Slider D = (v_g - v_p)/|v_p| sets the dispersion regime. D < 0 is normal (the carrier outruns the envelope, blue dot pulls ahead of the orange one). D = 0 is non-dispersive (the dots stay locked together). D > 0 is anomalous (the envelope outruns the carrier, the orange dot pulls ahead). The crests visibly drift through the packet in the dispersive cases.
