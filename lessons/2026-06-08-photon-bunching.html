A photon-counting simulator. Pick a source: thermal light from a vast number of independent emitters, coherent light from a laser well above threshold, or antibunched light from a single quantum emitter that can hold only one excitation. The upper panel shows the most recent clicks on the detector. The lower panel histograms pair coincidences as a function of delay τ between successive clicks, normalized so the value at long delays equals 1 (the dotted gray line). Thermal grows a bump at τ ≈ 0 climbing toward 2 (the Hanbury Brown-Twiss bunching). Coherent stays flat at 1 (Poisson, no bunching). Antibunched cuts a hole toward 0 (a single emitter cannot release two photons in one excited-state lifetime). The τ_c slider sets the coherence time of the thermal source and the recovery time of the antibunched source.
