City A fixed at 40°N, 74°W (New York). City B at 40°N, slider longitude (Madrid sits at lon ≈ −4°; Beijing at lon ≈ +116°). The two paths leave A in different compass directions: the parallel heads due east, the great circle heads roughly northeast and the geodesic arc bends back down to the same latitude at B. The further apart the cities, the further north the geodesic strays and the larger the savings.
