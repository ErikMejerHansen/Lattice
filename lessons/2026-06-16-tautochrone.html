Four beads slide along a cycloid arch under gravity, frictionless, from rest. Three are anchored at fixed starting positions (θ₀ ≈ 0.18π, 0.40π, 1.20π); the fourth (rust) is placed by the slider. Press release. All four reach the lowest point of the arch at the same instant, t = π√(R/g), regardless of where they started. Drag the slider, release again, and watch the arrival times stay locked together. This is Huygens' tautochrone property of 1659.
