A horizontal beam of white light enters the left face of a glass prism. Inside, each wavelength refracts to its own angle by Snell's law and Cauchy's formula n(λ) = A + B/λ². They exit the right face fanning into a spectrum that lands on a virtual screen at right. Widen the apex angle α to fan the colors farther apart. Switch material from fused silica (low dispersion) to BK7 crown (medium) to LF5 light flint (high) to watch the same beam spread more or less, even at the same apex. The readout shows n at the red and violet ends and the spread Δn that drives the fan.
