Two slits 0.3 mm apart at L = 1 m from a screen, illuminated at λ = 550 nm by a source whose angular diameter θ you set with the slider (logarithmic, 0.01 to 35 arcmin). The top panel plots the visibility envelope V(d) = |sinc(πdθ/λ)| as the slit separation d varies; the dot marks the actual d = 0.3 mm. The bottom panel shows the resulting fringe pattern on the screen, with peak-to-trough contrast equal to V. Drag right and the source widens; the spatial coherence length ℓ_s = λ/θ shrinks past d, and the fringes fade. Keep going and you reach the angular size of the sun (about 30 arcmin), well past several zeros of the sinc.
