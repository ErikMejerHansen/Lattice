As n grows, sin(nπx) oscillates ever faster. The strong-norm bar stays at √(1/2): in L² distance the sequence is permanently far from zero. The weak-pairing bar tracks 1/(nπ), the integral of f_n against the linear test function (1 − x), and shrinks to nothing. The same sequence is weakly null and strongly not. This is the asymmetry the direct method exploits.
