A free-particle Gaussian wave packet in natural units (ℏ = m = 1, initial width σ0 = 1). The thin red line in the upper panel is Re ψ(x, t), the carrier ripples. The filled rust curve in the lower panel is |ψ(x, t)|², the probability density (Born's reading from section 5). Drag k₀ to set the central momentum and t to scrub forward in time. Watch the envelope drift at the group velocity vg = k₀ while the carrier inside slides at vp = k₀/2: the ripples crawl backward relative to the blob. Set k₀ = 0 and the packet stands still but still widens, because Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2 means a position-localized state cannot also be momentum-localized.
