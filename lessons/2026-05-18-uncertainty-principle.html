Left panel: a Gaussian wave packet |ψ(x)|² in position, with width σx. Right panel: its Fourier transform |ψ̃(p)|² in momentum, with width σp. The slider sets σx (in arbitrary length units, with ℏ = 1). As you shrink the packet in x the momentum distribution broadens; as you widen it in x the momentum distribution narrows. Their product σx·σp stays pinned at exactly ℏ/2: the Gaussian is the unique state that saturates the Heisenberg inequality. Any non-Gaussian wave packet sits strictly above this bound.
